library(ggplot2)
met_avg_filtered <- met_avg[!is.na(mean_wind_sp) & !is.na(mean_dew_point)]

ggplot(met_avg_filtered, aes(x = region, y = mean_wind_sp)) +
  geom_violin() +
  labs(title = "Distribution of Wind Speed by Region",
       x = "Region",
       y = "Mean Wind Speed")
ggplot(met_avg_filtered, aes(x = region, y = mean_dew_point)) +
  geom_violin() +
  labs(title = "Distribution of Dew Point by Region",
       x = "Region",
       y = "Mean Dew Point")

In wind speed by region, we are seeing a wider range of wind speeds in the SW region. In the NW region, we see that there seems to be a higher distribution of mean wind speeds when compared to the SE and NE regions which have wider lower ends of the violin plot.

In the mean dew point by region, we see an equally wide range with NW and SW mean dew points. The SE has the highest distribution of mean dew points.

ggplot(met_avg_filtered, aes(x = mean_wind_sp, y = mean_dew_point, color = region)) +
  geom_jitter(width = 0.2, height = 0.2) +
  stat_smooth(method = "lm", se = FALSE) +
  labs(title = "Association Between Dew Point and Wind Speed by Region",
       x = "Mean Wind Speed",
       y = "Mean Dew Point")
`geom_smooth()` using formula = 'y ~ x'

The NE, NW, and SE regions all have slightly positive slopes meaning that as mean wind speed increases, the mean dew point increases. The SW region, however, has a negative slope. The SW region overall has more variability as the points are spread further away from the regression line both positively and negatively. The SE and NE region have tighter clusters of data points around the same area and are closer to the regression line.

library(leaflet)

met_avg_filtered <- met_avg[!is.na(mean_rh)]

top10_humidity <- met_avg_filtered[rank(-mean_rh) <= 10]

pal <- colorNumeric(palette = "YlGnBu", domain = met_avg_filtered$mean_rh)

leaflet(met_avg_filtered) %>%
  addTiles() %>%
  addCircleMarkers(~mean_lon, ~mean_lat, color = ~pal(mean_rh), radius = 5, fillOpacity = 0.8, stroke = FALSE) %>%
  addMarkers(data = top10_humidity, ~mean_lon, ~mean_lat,
             popup = ~paste("Top 10 RH: ", mean_rh, "%")) %>%
  addLegend(pal = pal, values = ~mean_rh, title = "Relative Humidity (%)",
            position = "bottomright")

The relative humidity across the US shows higher humidity along the coasts/densely populated locations. The least populated is the Western half of the US.
